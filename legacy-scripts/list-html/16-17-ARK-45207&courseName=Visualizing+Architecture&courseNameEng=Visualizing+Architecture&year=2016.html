Lukuvuosi 2019 - 2020,  Periodit I  The Innovative Strategies in Architectural Design is a research and design course, that links theory and site research to an architectural intervention. In the fast changing world, it is essential to learn how to search information and to become acquainted with some of the ongoing trends and their effects on the physical environment. Moreover, it is crucial to understand the relation between the person (the user) and the built environment. Nevertheless, essentially every architect should find one's own "niche", field of interest to deepen one¿s knowledge. Hence, this course focuses both on research and design, offering both practical and theoretical viewpoints for inspiration and information. The course aims to provide students who are preparing for their thesis with a theoretical base regarding Architectural Design, and challenges them to take a position within this discussion by means of analysis, design and critical writing. Firstly, students will work in small groups and get acquainted with research methods for site analysis, they will engage in critical discussion about theory and they will investigate contemporary programs. Based on the conducted site research, the strategic brief, and theoretical investigations, students design their individual area plans. They will continue on defining a concept and preliminary functional brief for experimental design. They will formulate their own individual approach for an architectural design based on the strategic briefing and area plan. The course is the first of the two advanced courses in Architectural Design followed by the course ARK-05227 Experimental Architectural Design. We highly recommend to take both of the courses in the same academic semester.
