Lukuvuosi 2017 - 2018,  Periodit II-III  NOTE: we have increased the seats for this course and form three exercise groups to overcome space limitations in our PC class. TUT, Nokia and Synopsys are organizing a special hands-on seminar course on modern digital ASIC design for master's and post-graduate students. The need for ASIC professionals is rapidly increasing in Finland, and passing this course opens excellent job and master's thesis opportunities in Nokia in spring 2018. This is also a great opportunity to learn from professionals in the field! To attend this seminar course, you should already be familiar with FPGA and digital design flow. The course concentrates on the process of producing ASIC designs ready for fabrication from RTL designs. Preferred pre-requisite courses are TIE-50106 Digital design and TIE-50206 Logic Synthesis, or corresponding skills in digital design. On the course, professional ASIC design tools from Synopsys are used together with modern technology libraries (32/90nm). A hands-on design case is worked on and processed throughout the course. The course covers all the essential stages and topics of a modern ASIC design: RTL Synthesis to Netlist Design-For-Test (DFT) and Automatic Test Pattern Generation (ATPG) Floorplanning Place and Route Formal Equivalence Checking Timing constraints and Static Timing Analysis (STA) Chip Finishing (extraction, Design Rule Checking (DRC) and LVS) The course consists of two 3-week intensive periods with lectures and exercises. The timetable of the course is somewhat flexible, as this is an extra course. This course is worth 8 credits.

Lukuvuosi 2017 - 2018,  Periodit III-IV  Visualizing software development. This seminar presents current approaches to implementing visualizations for software development data. We will go through issues related to data mining (gathering software development data, such as issues, bugs, tasks and test results from different tools), modeling it and choosing appropriate visualization techniques. We will discuss how visualizations can be used to help software development processes. The seminar combines a traditional seminar approach of familiarizing with state-of-the-art research on the topic and getting hands-on experience in visualizing software development. Teachers will provide an introduction to the topic. Students are expected to prepare a presentation on a scientific article or other named material on the topic (individually). Students are also expected to implement a small visualization of software development data (in pairs). Schedule: 3rd period: 2 meetings reserved for introduction/tutorial on the topic. 4-5 seminar meetings are reserved for student presentations on literature. 4th period: 4 seminar meetings are reserved for student presentations on visualization projects. This execution of the seminar will give 3 ECTS.

Lukuvuosi 2017 - 2018,  Periodit IV  User Experience in Robotics. After passing this course, the student can identify the important aspects of user experience in human-robot interaction and apply this knowledge in robotics design. The student is also familiar with the current research in robotics and can write a scientific report of their own design work. Topics covered on the lectures include basics of human-robot interaction, collaborative robotics, social and service robots, persuasive robots, cross-cultural aspects of robotics, and user experience of programming and controlling robots. The exercise sessions focus on hands-on user experience and interaction design activities as a part of a group assignment, which leads the student through a process of robotics concept design and evaluation. A student must have taken a course on the basics of user experience, such as "TIE-04100 Käyttäjäkokemuksen perusteet" or equivalent course in English prior to this course. The course "TIE-40106 Psychology of Pervasive Computing" is highly recommended.
