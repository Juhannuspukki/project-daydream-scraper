Repose to comment on KEB-23206 on Kaiku Dear Students, First, a few words about Class 2016: in general, the class was motivated and energetic and we enjoyed giving the lectures. It seems from the comments that most of you found the course well balanced, constructive, and even entertaining. There were also comments on the 2-half exam system, which I would like to respond to. The purpose of the half exam system is to divide the course material into more manageable loads and give a chance to the students to complete the course successfully. We started this policy 3-4 years ago, and the feedback has been very positive, especially from students who are not present in Tampere for more than 2 periods, and who don’t have a chance to sit for the later full exams. The system is also flexible as we offer also a full exam along with the 2nd half exam as well as two-full exams in the periods following the course period. There was one comment on the length of the questions of the half exams. The half exams are 3 h, the same as a full exam, but the material to read is half that for a full exam. Now we have doubled the time for the exam; that is, more topics can be covered in the two-half exams than is possible with one exam. We are not giving more questions per exam (the number of questions and the time allocated to answering them is the same as in a full exam, i.e., neither too few nor too many). Simply, in 3 h you can answer 6-8 questions of an appropriate length, whereas in two three-hour exams you will be able to answer at least 10-12 questions and thus increase your chance of success considerably. Thank you for your feedback and we definitely listen to your comments and try to incorporate your suggestions in the course when relevant.
