Vakiokysymykset

Mikä onnistui opintojaksolla hyvin?
Julkaistu palaute
Palautteen julkaisi: Hämäläinen, Timo, 25.04.2017
I think that the course is great. The teacher is excellent and so are the exercises.
The course is excellent.
The lectures were rich, they provide all the sufficient basics for the digital design engineer. And the computer exercises were really challenging and very interesting. I like this course and I like Timo also, he's one of the best lecturers I met at TUT. The only problem I and my international class mates faced that the content is really concentrated and the workload was extremely high comparing to the other courses that have also 5 credits.
Thanks to Prof. Timo Hämäläinen, presentation of lectures were great and attractive. In fact the most part of learning was done during the lecture sessions. And also thanks to teacher assistants Panu, Sakari and great thanks to Arto Oinonen that was available all the time to help and answer students'questions. I really enjoyed this course.
The lectures were good and the lecturer gave many useful advice. The computer exercises were also really interesting.





Miten kehittäisit opintojaksoa?
Julkaistu palaute
Palautteen julkaisi: Hämäläinen, Timo, 25.04.2017
I would provide the solutions for the exercises, it would be very good for studying and understanding better everything.
It would be great to have access to the solution of the exercises.
I would like to mention that the exercises were very time consuming. Though I like them, but they affect the time of the other courses as we use to spend at least 2 whole days per week to solve only the computer exercises. There are 2 suggestions: firstly, you can increase the credits of the course to 8 or 10 credits. Secondly, you can decrease the workload of the exercises. But I'm with keeping this amount of exercises, but increasing the number of the credits of the course.
It would be nice if some of the concepts related to RTL were explained in more detail in the slides. Giving the solutions of paper exercises would be a real plus because the exercise session is not enough to see what went wrong with our solution.
