Julkaistu palaute
Palautteen julkaisi: Jussila, Terho, 31.05.2017
There were 3 lectures every week which is too much content in 1 week. Attending only the lectures is not enough but with other courses it is near impossible to find time to study at home. The questions solved in the lectures were too generic. It would be better to have proper examples with one system model. At the moment with generic examples (double integrators etc) the solution was too mathematical. As an engineering student (not so good mathematical skills) it is like floating in the air if all focus is on mathematics. There should be some physical process so it is easy to visualize what is happening. It would be better to have one problem taken from the book and slowly make the problem complicated so the affects are clear. It is beautiful to see how math can be generalized but it became hard at some points. One thing that I would have liked to develop was how to approach a problem. This has unfortunately not happened in all my studies so far. In real life there are no question statements, no one asks what value to calculate so learning the skill of what to find and why to find it is very essential. In this course the approach to problems was very different. I felt it was designed for people working with control systems and the lecturer provided methods on how to solve their problems. For me the LQG control was very new and I was not prepared enough by taking the previous course. One thing I like is how every thing fell into one place in the last two weeks of the course. So I understood what LQG was about. Most things start to make sense but it would be nice if the picture was clear in the beginning also.
