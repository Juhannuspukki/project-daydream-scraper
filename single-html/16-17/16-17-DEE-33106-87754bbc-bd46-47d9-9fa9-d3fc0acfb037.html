Nykyinen vastaus
Dear All, Thanks a lot for the feedback. The first final exam was a great success but everyone should think about does the obtained grade really correspond to the mastering of the learning outcomes of the course - to be able to apply the information for analyzing any switched-mode converter? Some of the feedback is quite controversial, because somebody complaints that we advanced absolutely too slowly recapping things over and over, and somebody that the slow advancing was too rapid! I agree with the first one perfectly but as the feedback implies for most of the students even this slow speed is too fast. Think over this personally! Everyone shall understand that 5 credits requires work of 130 hrs. If lectures and exercises consume 35 hrs then the student shall spend personally 95 hrs at least. How many of you have done so? Somebody complained that it was difficult to get something from the lectures. That is absolutely correct and to obtain something the student has to work in advance and also understand the information given in the previous lectures. If this is not the case then it is naturally impossible to get anything from the lecturing and the exercises. I agree that most of the exercises are quite long but their intention is to show what the real work in power electronics really is. It is also highly recommended that the students try to solve them personally, and if having problems then the fully-solved examples will give information where the problems lie. The basic converters are treated in the previous courses already twice but still too many persons have problems in there analysis. It is difficult to imaging that the situation is like that. Does this actually mean that too many students have not intention to learn really something but only to pass the courses? I repeated over and over that the only teacher who can really teach yourself is the person her/himself. In the feedback, this is labeled as'shit talk' and 'we know that already'. The feedback implies, however, that the understanding is not materialized as actions. During the next academic year, a new lecturer will take care of the course. I thank everybody who has actively taken part of the lectures and exercises.
