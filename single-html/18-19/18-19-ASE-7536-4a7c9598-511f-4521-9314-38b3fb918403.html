Nykyinen vastaus

Aspects that need improving - more discussion in the tutorials and online - more modelling

Miten kehittäisit opintojaksoa?
Julkaistu palaute
Palautteen julkaisi: Piche, Robert, 30.05.2019
I believe 2 exercise sessions are redundant. I think the reason behind this is due to the required number of finished exercises to pass is quite high and students need to fulfill about those in order to partially get what is going on. I still believe 1 exercise is more valuable, students will have to sacrifice other courses if they do want to learn this course rigorously (they are all Master and PhD students, should know what is important to them). However, the other session could be the questions and answers between students and teachers. Students need to prepare for it and communicate with each other, answer other questions. This method, to me, is more suitable since this course is quite high level compared to other courses, students who choose this one really want to learn, not just finishing the course.
You could add some useful courses to the list of prerequisites in the study guide, e.g. optimisation and data analysis, since there is quite a lot of stuff one should know before attending the course.
The recorded videos had a little bit bad sound quality and sometimes it was anoying that I haven't seen what was written on the table.
I found the exercises too challenging and time consuming at least with my mechanical engineer background. Sometimes I felt like you should be a real mathematical genius to being able to solve the problems efficiently. For me it required a lots of work and still I couldn't get as many exercise done as the most of the other exercise participants. Especially exercise 11 was very challenging and it brought down the motivation to do exercise 12 problems because I had also lot's of other things to do. So as a summary, I would make the exercises more equally challenging and lower the required skill level. I waited for more practical explanations of the different algorithms. Too often I spend time trying to understand the slides which were full of complex mathematical equations and not much explanation what each symbol means. More explanations and practical examples. This is the second course where I have this 24 hours exam system and I don't like it at all. It is unfair to ask busy people to use so many hours of their day to do one exam on a one day. Why not just arrange 3 hour exam in a computer exam class to where anyone can go when they have suitable time slot for it.
On particular occasions, formulating the model was the key rather than utilizing a given model to estimate. I feel like the course content should include some modelling.
The subject matter is rather difficult, and I'm not sure the course does a good job of making the student to really learn it. Many of the exercises were about using and modifying previous code, understanding what one was doing was not that important. I think the object-oriented approach and ready code may be dangerous for learning, as most student's will take the path of least resistance and just try to get the exercises done with the minimum effort. Also, 2 periods is too long. By the end I had forgotten most of the basics already. Maybe it could be 1 period with more to do per week. I think it might be beneficial to cover the basics somewhat more. Things like Q,R,A,H,P matrices, where they come from, what do they look like for different models and to have more models. Now we mostly just used new filters for the same 2-3 models for the whole course with different filters and smoothers, so figuring out how the parameters etc. come with models was left a bit unclear.
No
12*6 exercises is too much work for 5 credits
