Nykyinen vastaus
Opettajan vastaus (JULKAISTU) : This was a successful experiment in intensive PhD-level "flipped-classroom" teaching with small groups. In spite of the relatively high work load, there were so many students that I needed to schedule 3 weekly tutorial sessions, and 17 students completed the course. The teacher work load was also high, but I enjoyed working with such talented and motivated students. The SLACK discussion board seemed to work reasonably well, although only a few students were posting. Students were able to find information from internet (e.g. mathematics stack exchange) and other sources, which is a useful skill for PhD-level students. I believe that the best feature of the course is the small group tutorial, where all students have the opportunity to present and defend their solutions.

Julkaistu palaute
Palautteen julkaisi: Piche, Robert, 22.01.2019
Teaching is very professional.
Exercise sessions were excellent.
A brilliant concept for a course. Given the opportunity, I'll surely attend similar courses again. The choice of material was also excellent.
This was one of the most educating courses I have attended, because it forced me to work hard and really think about the exercises.
The idea of using Slack for fostering problem-solving communication among the students was quite novel.
Slack board, book and exercise sessions formed a great combination. Especially the additional remarks and examples given were supplementing the book well and felt on point.
It was good that I learned overall studies fast
The self-pace type of implementation is very suitable for PhD students. It certainly takes time to complete the course but only having 2 hours of class per week is very convenient.



Miten kehittäisit opintojaksoa?
Opettajan vastaus :
I agree that spreading the work over a longer time would make it more accessible to students who have other time commitments. I agree that the number of credits is smaller than other Tampere University courses of equivalent work load, so it would be fair to increase the credit value. However, it may then be necessary to find another way to limit the course attendance to a sufficiently small number of competent and motivated students. I think that there is a need at Tampere University for this kind of PhD-level teaching, and the positive students' feedback supports this belief. I hope to have the opportunity to implement this and similar "core mathematics" courses in the future.
