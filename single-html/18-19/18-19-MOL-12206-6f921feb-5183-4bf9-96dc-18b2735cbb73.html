Nykyinen vastaus
This years implementation was mostly identical to the previous year, only the person responsible for the course changed. The number of lectures was slightly smaller as there are many lecturers on the course and fitting of the timetables is sometimes challenging. The course got overall rating of 4.07 and the appropriateness of the course in relation to the intended learning outcomes was considered as 3.92. Both grades are good and show that there are no major problems on the course. The workload was 1.93, which shows that the workload is suitable for the grades. The lectures were considered good and the topics were interesting. Some students would like to have deeper information about few topics while others would like to have even wider range of different topics. Making and presenting the poster was liked. However, some topics were considered too wide. The peer review was considered problematic as students do not have required skills to do that. More instructions about the evaluation might be needed and effect of the peer reviews on the grade should be smaller. In the next implementation, the number of the presented topics will be increased. Also the effect of the peer reviews on the grade will be evaluated and more instructions for the peer reviewing will be added. -Ilari Jönkkäri

Palautteen julkaisi: Jönkkäri, Ilari, 20.05.2019
Give some examples of what will the exam look like
The subject coatings for gaz turbine and coating for boilers are a little bit so vast. Maybe focus about a specifically subject according to this notion.
Less topics but more detailled
Sometimes I felt like while it is good to have a wide variety of material covered, depth may also be of importance. Hence maybe we could cover slightly fewer topics but go more in-depth for them.
forming of the groups was a little bit confusing. Also how the essays should look like was not clear in the beginning. An example or some generall expactations would be helpfull. The evaluation by students was interessting but some generell hints, how to evaluate would be nice, because every student has a different focus.
I think less general information, but more details about the important facts
I think the course is designed well giving different aspects of energy related materials. However, there was a lack of knowledge about advanced materials that are now being utilized,something related to graphene, materials involved in nuclear energy etc. I was also looking forward to get more information about renewable energy, but this was not the case entirely. I hope infuture more lectures will be based on renewable energy sources, as they are in current research trend.
some of the extern lectures were confusing and suitable - working together with the extern teacher about their context and lerntarget
The assignments during the course were poor. There was no instructions on what we were supposed to do, and the peer evaluation didn't work at all, especially since we were not given any evaluation criteria, and bad reviews were also counted. Some people could give best points for anything, meanwhile some could give poor evaluation only because they didn't fully understand lecture essay because "it was written in technical language" and the reviewer lacked skills in understanding English. In addition, it was difficult to share tasks equally in groups because some of them just want to pass and don't do anything for the group. I would give proper instructions for the assignments during next implementations, and the influence of peer reviews should be lower. Now 37,5 % of grade was given by peers, and since there were no evaluation criteria, grades seemed to be based on the person reviewing, not based on the content of the work. 20 % could be maximum influence of peer reviews.
