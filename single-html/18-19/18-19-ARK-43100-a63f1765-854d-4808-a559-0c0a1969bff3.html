Nykyinen vastaus
Dear students, Thank you for the feedback provided for the Architectural Design Professional Course (ARK-43100, ARK-43106). It will definitely help to develop the course in the upcoming academic years. Find here a common answer to the main issues. Although there was broad positive feedback too, the comments are focused on the aspects considered as improvable: Regarding the course theme, we were aware of the challenges with the project size from the very beginning. However, we considered it was beneficial for you to connect the course theme to the actual project to be realised in Helsinki. That is the reason why this year the task was a sort of exception in terms of size (total gross area) in relation to previous and following years. The fact of being a programme mainly consisting of exhibition premises, not so specialised as other building programmes (schools, kindergartens, libraries, hospitals), made us deem its suitability to support the learning process in your studies. We will consider future tasks involving more reduced building size in the future. Regarding the course structure, some of you considered the number of lectures was short, others that it was right. The lectures aim to give an overall view of the course theme at hand, to spark some inspiration on you and trigger your individual topics, and to get direct information from practitioners who have dealt with similar tasks in the form of competitions or built works in their professional activity. The practical rule with lectures was to avoid placing them the same days when studios were held, considering that the time prior to them is very valuable for you to ultimate your presentation (i.e. last sketches, printing). We will consider to increase its number, although not considerably since lectures are meant to support the design process as the main task. This is a controversial issue, because if there are too many lectures some students complaint about it alleging that they take too much time and what they really want is to have more time to discuss about their projects or just work on them. Please notice too that, due to time limitations, we have limited possibilities to enter in detail in some aspects that other courses should cover. However, we will consider adding more practical information in the lectures about the theme at hand. Regarding the course workload, you should know that we calculate it not considering individual situations in terms of office work outside the University or too many courses followed at the same time. The workload corresponds to a precise number of credits meaning an absolute number of hours, not relative (adaptable) to specific situations. That is how studies are planned and developed at the University. It is your responsibility to adapt other activities to your studies’ pace. This year we have noticed too many absences in studios and individual guidance sessions (even some no-shows after having booked the slots), which do not help in this sense. All teaching events are offered for the benefit of you and your learning process, therefore you should take the fullest advantage of them. Congratulations for your effort and results in general in the course. On behalf of the group of teachers, I wish you all the best in your future academic endeavours. Fernando Nieto, The Professor in charge of the course
