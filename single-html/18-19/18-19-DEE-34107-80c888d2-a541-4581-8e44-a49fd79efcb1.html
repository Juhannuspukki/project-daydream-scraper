Nykyinen vastaus
Dear students, Thank you for the feedback. As you known, this was the first implementation of this course and your feedback is valuable for improving the course. The course "Converter dynamics and EMC" is the substitutive course for this implementation. It was organized last year and has now been replaced by this course. Thus, there is no "gap". The PC test arose some feelings, both positive and negative, which is normal when you do things in a different way than in other courses. However, it seems that those who actively participated (and succeeded) in simulation tasks, got the best grades. Maybe in the next implementation I will add some more time to finalize the PC test and allocate more points to the simulation tasks. This year 20 % of the course grade came from simulation tasks, but maybe it would be fair to increase this to 30 % since a lot of work is put to simulations. The fact that you can take the PC test only once per semester gained some negative feedback, which I understand. However, I was very clear and straight about this from the beginning of the course. In countries with good technical education system like Germany you only recap exams once per year, which forces students to concentrate more on the topic during the course. The content of the PC test was very similar to what was trained during simulation tasks. Moreover, the lecture material was given. I will think about the possibility of having visiting lectures next year. Best Regards Tuomas Messo
