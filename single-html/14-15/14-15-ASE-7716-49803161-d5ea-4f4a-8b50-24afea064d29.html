Nykyinen vastaus
TEACHERS' RESPONSE TO FEEDBACK OF ASE-7716 Course Predictive and Fuzzy Control spring 2015 implementation got feedback from 22 students. The mean of "overall rating" was 3,81 and the mean of "appropriateness in relation to the intended learning outcomes" was 3,8. The numeric ratings as well as all answers to open questions have been published, so students can read each others' feedback from Kaiku. The seminars and group assignments got fairly good feedback. Most of the students considered hands-on Matlab work to be a good way of learning. Yet, a better linkage between the introductory lectures and exercises was desired. The course dealt with the same case example in all four assignments, which was considered good. The course was implemented for the very first time. This resulted in some problems with e.g. schedule and timing. Feedback to the group assignments was given too late, which clearly must be improved for the next implementation. Also, a summary after the seminars was desired. An obvious improvement is adding prerequisites. Now the course had no prerequisites at all, thus the participants had very different backgrounds. With the word control in its name, the course relied heavily on control theory. Students with less prior knowledge of control suffered from this. The answers contained valuable knowledge and suggestions for future implementations. We thank for the feedback and welcome you all to other advanced-level automation courses, such as Optimal and Robust Control with Matlab, Dynamic Planning with Incomplete Information, or Model-Based Estimation. With kind regards, Hannu and Mikko
