Nykyinen vastaus
Dear Students, Thank you for taking course KEB23206 in 2014 and for your feedback. We are glad to see that most of you not only did well, but also enjoyed the course. The purpose of the feedback is to listen to the students and see what worked well and what could be changed or upgraded to make the course better. Every new academic calendar we combine the feedback and our own experience in the class room and update the lecture, notes not only to address the needs of the students, but also to bring the lectures to up-to-date. One of the upgrades for 2014 was to expand the background material, which was found useful by many of you and too much by a few. Therefore, the plan for 2015 is to expand the background and put it on POP as additional reading to help with the relevant material downstream. We will be keeping many of the visual features, which from your comments seem very popular. Other changes that were effective and appreciated (e.g., in-depth assay on a particular topic and bonus questions) will be kept and we will try to include more practical material that fit into the course objectives. The use of abbreviations is unavoidable to lessen the amount of text in one slide, especially when words like enzyme, inhibition, substrate, etc. are repeated several times in one slide. We always explain the abbreviations when we introduce them for the first time, but in the future we will include a list of abbreviation for each lecture. This course has no book and that is why we have been developing the slides as much as possible to cover the important aspects of the course. We have been looking for a suitable up-to-date book for some time, but only recently we came across a couple of books that may be suitable as teaching material. Hopefully we will decide on the matter well ahead of the course implementation in period II. Whichever is the decision, the lecture notes will be upgraded as usual. Wish you all the best for 2015 and we hope that your stay at TUT was fruitful and enjoyable.
