Miten kehittäisit opintojaksoa?
Opettajan vastaus :
* About the close dealines of Active RF Circuits and RF Project. Yes I recognize the problem. I have a few alternatives: Option 1) I shall have RF project demos in mid november. Option 2) Active RF circuits presentations early on in 2nd period. Option 3) One of the courses shall have deadlines in 3rd period. All options lead to other problems: Problem 1) Tough for students to have circuits finilized in time. Practical work should rather follow the theoretical learnings... Problem 2) All students should be allowed to have sufficient time to prepare there presentations because this the most effective way of learning. Problem 3) In early January I'm busy with all preparations of another course, involving labs, for BSc students. * About the amount of credits: Your probably right. The work load was higher than I expected. It is interesting, however, that this was not an issue nine years ago (2005) when the course underwent a major reform and got its current form. I don't think I have added contents. Also I have major difficulties deciding which contents could possibly be dropped. Perhaps I'm just expecting deeper understanding. It is really difficult for me to accept that I should not expect that deeper understanding. Increasing the amount of credits, on the other hand, is not a straight forward act because it affects so many things. For example it might cause students to drop some other course they typically would take to complete their major or minor. If I keep the credits at 5 and stick to requiring the same deep understanding, I effectively filter out those students who would take this course just for the credits, those who care little about learning anything useful. Instead I would have a group of motivated students who are fun to work with. With small group of motivated students things turns out great and the co-operation often lasts beyond the course. On the other hand, as there are fewer students, my work is less cost effective and my employer doesn't like that. So I must balance between conflicting requirements... wish me luck!
