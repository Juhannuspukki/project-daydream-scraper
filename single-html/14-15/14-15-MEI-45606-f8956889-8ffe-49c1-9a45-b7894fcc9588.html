Nykyinen vastaus
I publish the feedback this course has received this far even though the questionnaire is still open for one week. As you can see, on average this course received quite positive feedback. Even though some complained about the project work being heavy, on average your opinion was that the workload on this course was similar to other 6 credit point courses at TUT. Regarding the question about previous knowledge, most of you answered 3 which is our target on the given scale: you knew something but you also learned something new. As usual, in the free form feedback there are both negative and positive comments from the same themes. Thank you for the feedback!

Diat ovat erinomaisia.
Harjoitustyö opetti käytännössä asioita verrattuna monien muiden kurssien teoreettisempaan näkökulmaan.
The course content and its implementation were very well explained in the introductory lecture. One particular problem was the expected report for the case product. The report structure was very comprehensive and understanding it from the very first week could have been more helpful for drafting the report. Although reports from previous years were handed out in the very first lecturers, the students couldn't make much out of it because the they had very basic ideas if any about what the content of every chapter should be. It might be easier to break the layout down in the first lecture slides more thoroughly before handing out the report samples. And I know that as Master degree students even handing out sample reports could be considered an extra favour because of the higher standard of studies but the final grading of the report proved very decisive towards the final grade of the subject. At the end may be the personal learning outcomes for every student was more or less in contrast to the final course grade. One other issue was the DFA tool and the introductory session. The program had various bugs causing crashes every now and then and navigating through its features was not particularly helpful and motivating towards understanding it. The introductory lecturer could also use more information on what exactly the DFA Index is, how its calculated and some clear quick examples of products and their assembly procedure which resulted in that particular index. The Lego example solved was helpful but having some more information before starting that could be more helpful. We were all told that the index should be within 10-30 for real life products but getting indexes in the range of 70 and much more shows that either the program wasn't working properly or the students didn't understood it.
