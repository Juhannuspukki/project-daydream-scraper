Nykyinen vastaus
Thank you all for your feedback regarding the course! We take the feedback into account when further developing the course. The feedback received was mainly positive. It is noteworthy that the course was successful in supporting the fulfillment of the learning outcomes and in forming a big picture for the purposes of the future working life. According to the feedback, the course credits and the workload seem to be in balance. On average, the workload was a bit smaller, but there was a significant variation in the actual working hours of individual students. The minimum workload for the course seems to be over 100 hours, but there are students that need/are willing to work more than the theoretical workload of the credit units. We are pleased that we received a set of constructive comments for the purpose of further developing the course. Such feedback is summarized in the following way. 1) Cases: The course consists of Harvard cases, talor-made cases and special events. The widely used Harvard cases received the best feedback. Based on the feedback, it should be ensured that the fruitful case work of the course is enabled with a multifaceted set of well-developed teaching cases. In autumn 2014, the three phase case was emphasized, and such a concept will be further developed both in terms of the structure and the content. The special events of the course received mixed feedback. This is a positive sign, it conveys the message that different events were valued by different students. The further development of the past special events (and the development of new ones) will be informed by the received feedback. 2) Final report: The final report, manager’s toolbox, was now used for the firts time, and it also received mixed feedback. The ability of the course to form a big picture for the future working life is somewhat related to the final report. On the other hand, the final report was considered as an ”extra task”. The specific requirements set for the final report were refined along with the course implementation, which quite naturally increased the perceived uncertainty related to it. On the other hand, the final reports were of a good quality, which clearly encourages keeping the final report in the course also in the future. 3) Course organization and communication: Course organization was on average at perceived to be at a good level. At the same time, there are avenues to further develop the course based on the feedback. In the BMC course, all the details of the implementation are not communicated to the students, to simulate the working life. However, we seek to reduce the unintentional uncertainty in the future. In the next implementation, for instance, the opening lecture can be clarified in terms of the course requirements. Thank you again for active participation in the course and have a nice spring semester 2015!
