Nykyinen vastaus
More detailed feedback will be published on course Moodle page on 9.3.2015. Partially because this was the last time this course was implemented we did not change anything – the course was organized in exactly same way it was implemented in 2013. From course staff, or at least my personal, point-of-view course went quite well. Similar to last year, grading the essays and assignments took too long. We are sorry for this. However, I think that at least in the early part of the course the feedback you got was more throughout than you will get here at TUT… In the free form feedback in Kaiku (and also on the paper sheets) some of you commented that grading was “harsh”. Thinking now, I have to admit that yes, probably the grading policy was a bit harsh. However, this year was the first time anyone commented about this and also you knew the grading policy at the beginning of the course – you might have commented something already in August or September… And now, after the course, looking at the course grades, the distribution looks quite OK. And everyone who submitted all tasks also passed the course! The distribution pattern was very similar also in 2012; in 2013 we had so few students that comparison is not meaningful. Some of you commented about the course schedule was heavy. Yes, it probably was. However, this was 6 credit point course so, according to guide lines, you should have spent 160 hours working on this course. And according to your feedback, most of you considered that the workload was not (at least a lot) too heavy. Furthermore, all course deadlines were given at the beginning of the course so that you can plan your own schedules. Finally, if this course would be given again, I think we would need to something for the following things: - Grading needs to be faster. However, I wouldn’t want to compromise the “quality” of the feedback we give to you. - More sample calculations, especially considering measurement uncertainty. - More support for working with NI Vision Builder. I don’t know about guided PC class room session, but I hope that the videos I made during the course were useful (although they probably came too late). - Essay topics need to be better formulated. I don’t want to give up using essays, because I think they are quite useful tool for making you actually think about some of the course topics.
