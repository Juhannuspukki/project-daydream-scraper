One problem with the course was that it was not very clear what is included on the course / what is required. It was said at the first lecture, that lecture slides are not enough but students have to read also books. HOwever, in the lecture slides of Amit Das, there ws a lot of material which was not dealed with during the lectures, but it was not totally clear if all theses things should be learned. In addition, these lecture slides contained severe mistakes (see below). We also get a long list of possible exam questions, which included many questions, which were not included in the lectures. If the student does not have much knowlegde about elastomeric materials beforehand, all this content in lecture slides and exam questions (which requires a lot of studying from books) exceeds clearly the 6 credit points. Some example od mistakes in lecture slides: -Lecture 3, origin of rubber elasticity, slide 4: Creep and recovery. In the figure below, at right there is text “recovery”, but this is NOT RECOVERY, this is RELAXATION. You say it yourself at slide 8: Recovery: Holding a sample at a set leghth, the change in stress as a function of time is recorded. -Sigma and epsilon: Lecture 3, e.g. slide 16. Sigma is stress (as usually) , small epsilon is strain (as usually) L5 Characterization: Epsilon is called stress and sigma is called strain. Why?? In addition, the structure of lecture slides (and lectures) of Amit Das is not easy to follow. In addition, terms were a problem at this course. One example was this "ultimate rheometric torque" -term used in excercises, which could not be found in any books or any materials. In exam questions there were a term "xantogenates" which was not found in any other material, but Google told that this is a german word for "xantate". But trying to find all these terms takes just too much time. If you give this question list, please go through the questions and terms. If I consider all these things I explained, I think the workload of this course is rather 7-8 points than 6 points. This can be helped by making the structure of lecture slides more clear, eliminating the mistakes, going through the question list, and making clear how much should be read from books.
