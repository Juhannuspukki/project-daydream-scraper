Amount of credits is always a difficult question specially when the background of students is very heterogeneous at the course. Students having electrical engineering or smart grids background might feel course relatively simple, but students outside electrical domain are lacking basic knowledge and understanding of electrical circuits and systems. Therefore the workload might be very different for the students. Prerequisities listed on course description really describe the expected knowledge of students in the beginning of the course. The average of workload seem to be OK, because majority of students are coming from electrical domain. However information from students coming outside of electical domain is very welcome to fully understand the level of knowledge they have in the beginning of course.

Miten kehittäisit opintojaksoa?
Opettajan vastaus :
Finnish excercises will be considered next year. EU / Nordic focus on course will be continue due to major differences of regulation, market, etc. between EU and USA or Asia. For example in EU network company may not own generation units while this is possible elsewhere. This has a dramatic change how networks are planned and operated. Of course international students might be interested how these things are in USA or Asia, but everything might not be included for a single course. Credits of the course are remarked within previous questions. Next year simulation excercises will be evaluated and therefore they will also have impact on grade. Scheduling of all courses of department is a challenging task because overlapping of courses is needed to reduce as much as possible. It is also well known challenge that sleeping schedule of most students and normal working hours of university does not meet very well. Due to these reasons the teaching of several courses will start at 8 am also in the future. The university is following the general schedule of Finnish society, therefore changes here are very difficult.
