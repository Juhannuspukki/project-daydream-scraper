Nykyinen vastaus
Big thanks for everyone for the feedback you gave me. You did much hard work to pass the course. Thanks also for the feedback during the course since some of the assignments had some errors in it which are hard to spot if you have done them yourself. Here is my response to your feedback: -The workload is quite high compared to other courses since this is one of the few practical courses we have in our department. Therefore, there is no reference where to compare the amount of work you have done. However, I will intend to keep the workload as it is. I believe that learning by doing is the best way to make you better engineers. Yes its tough, but you can expect it to just get more tougher when you graduate. -I will try to make the task explanations more detailed next year as there were some misunderstandings what to do in each task. This was the first time the course was implemented during two periods do I expected some difficulties like this. -Passing the course requires some background knowledge on switched-mode converters. Courses DEE-33106 and DEE-34106 are recommended prerequisities. I will consider making the compulsory prerequsities in the following implementations of the course. -Covering the dynamic properties of a boost converter during one lecture is quite impossible. However, I should have given some time to explain why the capacitor was increased to lower the resonant frequency to 400 Hz. Lowering the resonant frequency by increasing capacitance was essential to make the converter controllable by other than I-type controller. -I will consider adding some material on the placement of components. I know it is not a trivial task. -I did not publish the feedback regarding the last question since the feedback was supposed to be anonymous. However, it seemed that most of you learned some new skills or at least were able to strengthen the skills you already have. That is very pleasing to hear. Knowing what exact skills you need in the industry is impossible since the vast variability of topics. However, most propably you will need to learn something you have never done before, e.g., new computer program, new topologies, new materials & components etc. So be prepared to learn anything new that advancing on your future career requires.
