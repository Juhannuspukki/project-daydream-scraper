Nykyinen vastaus
Thank you everyone for the valuable feedback. It helps to develop and improve the course in future. Most of the criticism seemed to focus on 1) the course being in the 4th period; 2) the course duration being only one period instead of two; 2) an inadequate amount of guidance; and 3) the workload When I took over arranging this course, I was aware that the 4th period is not the best timing for the course (is it good for any course?), but it was out of my hands at that time due to unexpected changes in personnel. As soon as the situation with personnel recruitments stabilizes, the course will likely return to its planned location in the 1st period. I totally agree with you that it would be better to arrange the course during two periods instead of one. Unfortunately, the tendency in the department is the opposite; in the coming semester, professors will experimenting with arranging even major (10 cr) courses in just one period. As for the inadequate amount of guidance, this was also something I was aware of in advance. I would also have preferred to have at least three guidance sessions, but there was not much we could do with just two teachers and with the Easter and May Day occurring on our lecture day (Friday). Thanks to your feedback, the number of guidances will be increased to three next year. When it comes to the workload, the fact that many of you experienced it as excessive may be connected to the short duration of the course and the simultaneous workload from other courses. According to calculations, the amount of work should not be unreasonable for master students (=nearly ready architects) when done in pairs. I believe the previous experience with renovation or the lack of thereof also affects how the workload is perceived. In addition, problems may occur if the workload is not divided equally between the members of the team. The submitted projects also illustrate the excess of the workload because many pairs had treated the reuse part of the task quite lightly. Next year, I will pay attention to reducing the workload. In general, I can say from the teacher's perspective that the main challenge in arranging this course is the fact that you students have immensely diverse backgrounds and we teachers know absolutely nothing about them. It is very hard to know what to teach you and what you might already know. This year, it seemed that this was the first renovation course for the most of you, which was a bit of a surprise. Nevertheless, it's been a nice experience to work with you and I wish you all the best in your studies and later life. Have a nice summer! Satu
