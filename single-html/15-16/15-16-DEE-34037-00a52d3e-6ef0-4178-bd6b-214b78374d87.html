Nykyinen vastaus
Thank you all for the valuable feedback and big thanks for our course assistant MSc Aapo Aapro for managing this year's implementation. Getting hands-on experience with power electronics is very important and appreciated by employers. We are dedicated to improving all the courses offered by DEE and try to take all comments into account. Some issues that shall be fixed before the next term: -Equipment is poor and we know this. We should invest to a new soldering station and equipment. -Discussion has been started about giving a grade on the course. However, this is not an easy task since everyone has totally different background and the grade should reflect how much the student has learned and worked during the course. The idea of the course is to emphasize learning essential skills, not improving GPA. However, those who work a lot should be rewarded somehow. -Control design related to the course should not be too difficult if one has completed DEE-34106 Converter Dynamics and EMC which is a compulsory pre-course. We have to have an internal discussion if the precourse provides enough basis for completing this course. And I also want to stress that power electronics, in fact, is an important application area of control engineering. Nowadays you cannot categorize engineering problems to have a strict field but everything is connected. -There should be a possibility to make more demanding circuits and more specific tests.
