Best course ever
The grading doesn't incentivise learning, it incentivises effort. The only parts of the grading that actually reflects the student knowing something are the exam -- which is almost completely ignored -- and the programming assignments. Homework is useless, being present during sessions/lectures is even more so, the lecture essays are repetetive at best and menial most of the time, and ByTheMark exercises -- while good in and of themselves -- are trivial to complete. The exam can be passed essentially with some cursory knowledge on the subject, but a high grade would've required more effort than I've put in any course up until now. The alleged virtues of this system are just not present; the lecturer keeps claiming you can choose how you want to study, but that's clearly false, as you need to study in every single way in order to get a high grade. Maybe this will raise the percentage of passing students for this course (which would explain why you've picked this course to experiment with), but if a university student can't pass this course without being forced to study, they just shouldn't.
The last homework assignment was very hard in my opinion and overall, the slides for the programming assignments lacked explanations a bit. Also, having to pass every programming assignment in order to pass the course was a bit too strict. Additionally, I think the final exam should have affected the final grade.
