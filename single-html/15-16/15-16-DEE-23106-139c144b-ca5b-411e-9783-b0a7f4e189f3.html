Nykyinen vastaus
The course is the first one in the Smart Grids MSc program. The assumption is that all students in the course are bachelors in electrical engineering and that they have the basic knowledge on electrical engineering and also control theory. During the course it turned out that the assumptions on the skills of the students before the course were not realistic (e.g. I was assuming that per unit system is taught everywhere already at the very first power engineering courses but this seems not to be the case). Hence, the course is in its current form too difficult for many students and we will need to make some changes to it for next year. The other side here is that it is not possible to start from the very basics since the course is a MSc course and we need to be able to assume that all MSc students know for instance how to calculate with complex numbers etc. I will probably try to make a list of things that should be known before coming to the course for next year so that the students can restudy the things that are necessary to know in this course. The schedule of the exercises received some earned critique. I agree that the time between the lecture and the exercise was too short and also the exercise materials should have been published earlier. This will be changed next year. Several students wanted to have more exercises and solved examples. This will most probably be realized next year and we are also planning on adding some computer assignments to the course.
