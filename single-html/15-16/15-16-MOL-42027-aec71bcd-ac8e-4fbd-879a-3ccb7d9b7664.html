Nykyinen vastaus
In 2016 the normal lectures were partly replaced with flipped classroom –teaching. Four flipped sessions were organized of which two were compulsory, and extra points could be obtained by participating each of those. The students were given a chapter from a book which should had been studied/glanced before the lecture. During the lecture times the students were divided into smaller groups and few problems relating to the subject were given to be discussed/solved, and lecturers were there to help if needed. After the session students individually wrote short summary of the problems. The flipped classroom teaching was more interesting than the conventional lectures, the same feedback was obtained from the lecturers and the students. However, in future the preliminary material should be processed somehow so that at least the key things of the specific subject can be picked/understood more easily by the students. Now if a student did not use much time for the self-study some gaps could easily be left from the basics. In the course three mid exams were held, which was seen better than the more normal amount of two. Part of a mid-exam was multiple choice questions which were seen too difficult and specific by the students. In future the questions should be modified to be more abstract or to exclude these fully. The general grades from the exams were slightly lower than during the previous years, partly due to those multiple choice questions but maybe partly also due to the change in the lecturing technique. The flipped classroom technique requires more from a student, so that he/she should use more time for self-study compared to normal lectures. If a student acts similarly as in normally lectured course (comes to a classroom only to listen the lecturer, and finally reads the material before the exams ), it can easily be that the state of learning is lowered. The feedback of the course was however generally good or very good. The flipped classroom teaching was seen as very good and interesting by most students. Of course some students were not that pleased with that but the majority feedback was very positive. The lectures of the mechanical part were seen as difficult to follow. Next year this part will be modified so that either clearly more time is used to this with eg. some example exercises, or then the level of the theory that should be learned will be lowered. Now too much were tried to be told in too little time, and it was not seen as a good thing. In future the flipped classroom teaching will be used but maybe so that alternately there will be a normal lecture and then a flipped session. In that way the possible gaps in the theories/subjects will not be that likely, and more consistent understanding of the key things of the course will be improved. Olli Orell 1.7.2016
