peofessor are very nice

Studio groups concept
The implementation of the competition target in the development of the course.

I liked the possibility to get individual guidance quite often and to hear different opinions. I enjoyed studios a lot and the lectures were different from one another and interesting.
I enjoyed the topic of accessible housing as well as the studio interaction. Particularly the day of the mid-critique in which we discussed as a larger group how to address the needs of different individuals. I really appreciated that the final submission and presentation was digital! This saved a lot of time and money on such a short project deadline.
The guindances and the studios.
personal guidances were great - I took guidances from Sini and Jari, they were always so nice and helpful

- I liked the studio system in interaction with the lectures - I liked the way teachers were encouraging students

The importance of encouraging students to improve their graphical design due to a better project comprehension.

My biggest regret is that I missed quite a lot of Markku's lectures due to overlapping schedule. I wish there were some lectures to see and discuss existing projects that address accessibility in different way. It would also be nice to have more days for the course, not only Mondays and Tuesdays since it motivates more to stay put and continue working on the project until the end of the week. And the studious were nice, I wish there were more of them.
This studio layout is very different from my home University and was difficult to get use to. At my home University we have studio 3 hours a day, and 3 times a week (Monday, Wed. and Friday). There has probably been lots of feedback on this already, but I feel that the course was too complicated and rushed that I feel like wasn't able to develop my design or research the site enough. One thing I also didn't like, was being so separated from the rest of the group. I wish we could mix studio group and be able to see other student's projects outside of our studio group. I think the individual guidance was important, but it was difficult to see different professors because the first part of the 20 minutes is spent just describing your project and I felt the feedback became really general. It was also difficult to see a new professor, because if they were popular they were always full. I wish we had more time during the week to discuss each other's projects as students. At my home University we all are working together in the same space for 3 hours and so it's easy to go over and discuss with a fellow classmate or professor about your project. Here I found there isn't much of a studio environment, people for the most part work separately. I also found that students were more isolated from one another on this project, because each design, scale, and site was completely different. I think there should be some common ground. I also think this should be a semester long course.
Maybe the lectures could be more actives. Also I think there is no time to react or change your project if you need it. In my opinion a project is a work in progress all the time, so it is important to have enough time to develop it
I have to say that I did not enjoy my studio meetings. I hardly got any feedback from the teacher all the time, I was glad that I could go during the personal guidances to other teachers...
Not make the course of the seven-week class. I think it is a horrible idea to have Design Studio taught in 7 weeks. The intent to try to make student more productive by only giving them seven weeks I think has no logic students will procrastinate no matter how much time they have. I personally do not procrastinate my work and I felt that this project was extremely rushed and decisions have been made to quickly which resulted in a poor project . I thought the lectures were wonderful however how is one supposed to apply what is being taught in the lectures when you only have two weeks before the projects due. . I would put the lectures the first period with site Analysis and second period design work.
It might well be a typically Finnish feature (not the first project I observe it), but for me as a foreigner student time is divided a bit unevenly in a sense that there's much of talk...and talk...and talk...and then suddenly you have a MID-review =) I already know that Finnish students are very fast producing good pics/plans/layouts...whatsoever, while for me it is very important to keep working steadily, at more or less the same pace right from the beginning. Thus, it might be necessary to remind us, students, from time to time that architects speak in drawings...perhaps
- lectures about essentials like floor plans could take place a bit earlier during the course - do not be afraid to express criticism straight forward
