Nykyinen vastaus
Response by the instructor, Waqar Hussain. Original feedback to which the responses refer to are in quotations. "Project work and exam should be more different" I kept it a bit similar so that students can pass the exam easily and the exam could cover a wide range of topics. "A bit more structure would be nice. I was lost most of the time, not due to complexity, but lack of order." It is the second round of implementation, thank you for your comment and we will keep it more structured in the future. "Give more support material." The course covers the latest research topics and it is hard to recommend a text book for it. However, many similar research articles can be searched from IEEE, Springer and Elsevier databases. "Material was not good for studying. Mostly information had to be digested from lectures or find material on my own. Examination was not good, because "bring all non-electronic material to succeed" is not working in my opinion. Exam should measure general knowledge about the topics (big picture) instead of measuring the amount of useful material you bring to the exam..." In this course, attending all the lectures was mandatory and it was said in the beginning of the course, if a participant of the course misses a lecture, he/she should schedule an appointment with the instructor to discuss the contents of the lecture so that in the next lecture, the participant and the instructor are on the same page. The examination was just an expanded version of the project exercise and since almost all the students did well in the project exercise, I wonder why a few refused to attempt the examination at all while making this an excuse. Only a prior knowledge of matrix-vector multiplication and Fast Fourier Transform was required which is basic and it is expected that the participants should know about it. The exam is certainly covering a very large picture which includes the design of a multicore architecture where different heterogeneous cores are communicating/processing over a Network-on-Chip (NoC) and each heterogeneous core is a tailored to the processing requirements of an application by extending its instruction-set architecture. The amount of written material you bring to the exam is certainly useless unless a participant has actively and been mentally present in the class and interacting with the instructor. "The course should have exercise sessions. And the quality of the slides should be improved. And the course should be based on textbook not based on badly written or explained research papers." In future, more exercise sessions will be included and the quality of slides will be improved. However, the instructor aggressively uses the white board so the communication with the students is more interactive than just passing/changing the slides and don’t care if a student absorbs the idea or not. The scientific databases (IEEE, Springer, Elsevier) are full of quality research articles that are similar to the ones recommended by the instructor. It was not mandatory to follow only the ones that the instructor published after a strict peer-review. "-Luennot olivat sekavia, luentokalvoja ei juurikaan käytetty tai ne olivat huonosti tehtyjä -Itseopiskelumateriaalia ei käytännössä ollut, koska kalvot olivat suoraansanottuna surkeita -Oppimistavoitteet olivat epäselvät -Tenttiin sai tuoda materiaalia, mutta tästä ei ilmotettu etukäteen kuin ilmeisesti sivulauseessa kesken luennon. -Htyön tehtävänanto oli epäselvä. Kokonaisuutena kenties huonoin kurssi, jonka olen TTY:llä käynyt. Aihe oli kiinnostava, mutta en oppinut kurssilla mitään uutta. Tenttiin lukeminen oli mahdotonta, koska materiaalia ei oikeastaan ollut. Kurssikirjaakaan ei ollut, eli siitäkään ei voinut asioita opiskella." This email was sent to all the participants of the course which is as follows Title: ELT-44606: Final Examination Text: Dear Students Today we conducted oral evaluation of your project exercises and a few of you came up with elegant solutions, specially 255960 and 255757. I really appreciate them. In the last lecture, I would like to have your collective input related to the topics of your interest to draft the final exam which can evaluate the best of everyone. In this context, I would ask you to think meanwhile and come up with potential questions that needs to be addressed. In this way, the final exam will be an entity containing an equal but distinctive share from all participants. Best regards Waqar Course: ELT-44606 Digital Circuits and Platforms Course implementation round: ELT-44606 2015-01 Time: Nov 26, 2015 5:35 PM Sender: Hussain Waqar Target: Course implementation Almost all the students came to this lecture to give their opinion about the exam and the exam was designed based on the mutual consent of the participants. All the participants were well informed about how the exam will be constructed which can judge and evaluate the best of everyone. The international students (Italian and Spanish) have done tremendously well and they are still at TUT, they can be invited for interview to describe their comments about the course. The participants collectively decided to keep the exam ‘open resource’ irrespective of the opinion of the instructor. The students that missed the final lecture didn’t not communicate with the instructor to know the collectively decided format of the final exam. However, the criticism provided in the feedback will be taken positively and steps will be considered in the next implementation round so that no such situation is observed.
