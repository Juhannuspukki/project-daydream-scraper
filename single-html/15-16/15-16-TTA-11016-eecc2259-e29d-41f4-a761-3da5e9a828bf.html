Nykyinen vastaus
Hei, Thank you for your valuable feedback on the course. I have used the flipped classroom method & week exams for about three years now. Therefore, the feedback does not really provide too many surprises to me – some students like it some do not. However, since the majority of the students appreciate the learning opportunities such approach provides and consider the workload appropriate, it gives me confidence to keep developing the course further in this direction. This year I finally was able to have a complete manuscript of the book – with Mr. Möttönen making on Exit in the end. Thanks to the positive feedback of the narrated textbook, my department has provided me the funding for language check. Thus, next year the language used in the book will be much more professional, without spelling and grammar errors. Based on the student feedback, I will prepare more exercise material with animated solutions for the students to have a possibility to practice the core exercises more and, in that way, build routine and gain some confidence for the week exams. Many of the advanced exercises I have prepared are actually based on the business cases I have been involved in with different companies, and I intend to include those stories (i.e. narratives) I tell in the class also in the animations. Finally, to motivate students for independent studying, I am planning to require students to fill in a “study log” on a weekly basis. Such approach would (1) make it visible for the students (especially those not familiar with independent studies) that students are indeed expected to use time outside the class room for learning (4 cu = 110 hours of work). Such approach would also (2) help students set a target for each week exam and then plan the studies accordingly. Finally, such a study log would (3) enable interesting analyses regarding the time invested in learning and the exam outcome – making it visible that (usually) time invested in studying has a strong correlation with the performance.
