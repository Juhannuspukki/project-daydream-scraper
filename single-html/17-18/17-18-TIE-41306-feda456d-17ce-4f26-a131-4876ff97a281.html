Nykyinen vastaus
Thank you for making the effort to give feedback about the UXDE course! It's really nice to see such positive evaluations in general about the learning outcomes and increased capability for independent and critical thinking. From the teachers' perspective, this development was also evident during the course, so you can be satisfied about the work you've done. Your suggestions for improving the course are very helpful and will be taken into consideration. I've listed below the essential takeaways for what worked well and shouldn't be changed (marked with + sign), and for things to improve in the course (marked with - sign). Lectures: + Keep the number of lectures small and their content concise, and keep the possibility to watch them on video. + Arrange interesting guest lecturers. - Content-wise, reduce the basic UX definitions, and increase the discussion of UX models with concrete and practical examples and guidelines. This seems to be the largest improvement need on the course content: the actual use and benefits of UX models remained somewhat vague and wasn't explained well. - During lectures, have also practical exercises related to the theoretical parts. - Ensure that the guest lecturers' slides are recorded on video (if they're using Mac). Weekly exercises: + Keep the focus on practical work related to the group assignment process. - Connect also the latter exercise sessions more tightly to the group assignment. - Choose a timeslot later than 8 am for the exercise sessions :) Group assignment: + Collect topics for group assignment from external customers/companies. + Form the groups based on a preference survey in the beginning of the course. - Make sure that the customers are from Tampere region (students definitely should not need to travel for course work). - Add a question about motivation (the grade you would be satisfied with) to help in forming balanced groups. - Give an example of the whole group assignment process to explain more clearly how experience-driven design process is carried out on this course, and what are the steps and expected schedule for the work. Exam: - Explain what it means to write a reflective essay. Overall, the focus on experience-driven design approach will be maintained, as it provides a certain kind of a framework and perspective towards UX design. The vision of designing for an experience is important to grasp, even if it may not be so easy to outline UX goals and follow the experience-driven process when doing practical work in the industry. UXDE aims to provide understanding of the theoretical aspects of UX, while giving tools to apply theory into practice. Next year, the connection between theory (especially UX models) and practice will hopefully be more clear. Thanks again and hopefully you'll be able to use what you've learned in practical work!
