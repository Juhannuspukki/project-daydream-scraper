Nykyinen vastaus
Thank you for the feedback. I will answer instead of Matias and Ammar who were the actual lecturers. Based on my understanding many groups completed the project within the given time and there seemed to be a steady increase in knowledge. I hope you had a good experience and keep on increasing your knowledge and skills in power electronics. Right now it is a good field to be in... One student criticized the course of being "ancient". I am not sure what he/she meant. The way the converter looks like depends on the skills of the student, not the assistant or lecturer. Software tools used in the course (PADS & Simulink) are still standard in companies. Moreover, the used DSP is actually quite new and you are likely to see something similar in current power converters. Now, it does not really matter in this course if the MOSFET is old or not. We could use Silicon-Carbide transistors but then most of you would be struggling even more as SiC devices are very fast and likely cause more distortion to your measurement circuit. Moreover, the topology is simple due to a very simple reason: If you cannot get a simple Boost converter to work, you have no hope of building more advanced topologies such as e.g. dual-active bridge or interleaved bi-directional dc-dc converter. There were some problems with TUTLab, because it was not available as much as we had hoped. Maybe next year we will shift the course back to the department premises. This year there was a lot of work for Matias and Ammar because the DSP was renewed and a lot of new course material had to be generated and co-operation with TUTLab established. However, thanks for your patience! Best Regards Tuomas Messo Assistant Professor in Power Electronics
