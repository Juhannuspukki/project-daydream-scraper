Nykyinen vastaus
Thank you for your feedback! This was the first implementation of the course that lasted only for one period (previously the course lasted for two periods). I think that we had enough time for the basics of the measure and the integral (Chapters 2 and 3), but it seems that in the future we will have to choose between the Lebesgue measure on R^n and L^p spaces. I am not planning to write lecture notes, because Axler's book is very good for the course. Next year the book will have chapters also about the Lebesgue measure on R^n and L^p spaces. And hopefully I have time to publish the proofs that can not be found in Axler. There was an idea about exercises where you are asked to comment on or to fill the gaps in a given proof. This is an excellent idea! We sometimes use this method in some of the basic courses, but I will try this next year also in analysis courses.
