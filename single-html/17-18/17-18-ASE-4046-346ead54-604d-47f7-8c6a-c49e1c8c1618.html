Opettajan vastaus opiskelijoiden antamaan toteutuskertapalautteeseen
Nykyinen vastaus
Students are very satisfied: mean student evaluation grade is 4.1 for a course that for most students is not mandatory. Teaching innovations that were introduced this year seem to work well: 1. weekly EXAM tests (but some students had problems running out of time) 2. lecture recordings 3. course notes in the form of slides with detailed annotations Planned improvements: 1. increase allowed time for EXAM tests 2. improve the weekly exercises: improve attendance, provide more interaction among students and interaction with teachers 3. add Python as an alternative to Matlab

Mikä onnistui opintojaksolla hyvin?
Julkaistu palaute
Palautteen julkaisi: Piche, Robert, 13.06.2018
Pikatentit ja luentovideot.
The lectures of the first period were well structured and helpful for the learning outcome
It was very useful to have the recorded lectures available for self-study. Even though there were additional notes in the course slides, the recorded explanations gave an additional boost to learning the material.
Robert is a great teacher, enjoyed his classes.
Weekly exercises and exams
Osatentit vaikuttivat toimivalta ratkaisulta.
Excellent
I liked the weekly exam system. It was a good way to keep you busy and learn new things regularly. It was also nice that the lectures were recorded. I liked the arrangements and the style of the course overall too.
The optimization part
Weekly exams were good way to force to practice each part of the course. Also could say they were "a must" since there was so much things to learn. Material and exam feedback was good.
Harjoitukset hyviä. Luennot ja niiden videointi loistojuttu. Helpotti harjoitusten tekoa kun voi vielä kerrata luennon asiat videon avulla. Muistiinpanojen lisäys luentokalvoihin todella toimiva asia. Luentovideot + kalvot muistiinpanoilla todella tehokas lisä opetukseen.

Miten kehittäisit opintojaksoa?
Julkaistu palaute
Palautteen julkaisi: Piche, Robert, 13.06.2018
The difficulty curve on the course was exponential rather than linear and the end-game was out of control.
In my opinion the fourth period suffered from a lack of context between the individual lectures and it was too much material to go into detail. I would appreciate more theoretical background and graphical explanations. Therefore, I was able to pass the tests by practicing with the exercises, however I do not know if I could transfer it to other cases.
If there are some recommended books on the course topics, these could be mentioned in the lectures. There were some recommended books on optimization, but I didn't notice any on Bayesian inference.
Maybe less exams but also include a project, where you can see a progression.
Could be more examples and exercise questions. EXAM should have more time, too much time spent on just solving matlab syntax issues, felt more like battling the clock, than battling the questions.
More interaction
Although the weekly exams are definitely a good way to teach things in this kind of course, it may be possible that you forget some methods as quick as you learned them. Maybe replacing couple of exams with one larger exercise where you need to use and mix couple of methods in one bigger task would serve learning in longer terms better? Just a thought. Some problems in exams required more work than others and if you made a mistake, you didn't really have time to search for the reason of the error. So sometimes 50 minutes felt a little too short time for the exam. Some other times it was more than enough though.
Some more exercises with solutions for individual work (not mandatory), provide more basics for the data analysis part
For me, couldn't get as much out of lectures (and bit from exercises) as I wished to. One everlasting issue is time sharing between courses but at least one thought comes into my mind. - Could there be some overview material (couple slides) for each lecture to read before the lecture which could contain objective of the subject ("motivation", ie. for what Kalman filter can be used to) and some basic terms and concepts of the subject (like for the Kalman filter Ak is state-transition model and one sentece of what it models). Might give better understanding to lectures and which directly correlates to exercises. Weekly exams were really time consuming and laborious but couldn't see a good option for it. Maybe exam every two weeks when there is very similar subject.
Mielestäni 50 min Exam-tentin/harjoituksen tekemiseen liian lyhyt aika. Usein tehtävissä monta kohtaa, jolloin aikaa jäi n. 10 min per kohta. Usein tehtävät olivat kuitenkin tehtyjen harjoitusten muunnelmia jolloin koodia joutui muokkaamaan ja aika ei riittänyt debuggaamaan mahdollisia virheitä. EXAM-järjestely erittäin toimiva - pieni kehitysehdotus vain.
Harjoituksissa oli mielestäni huono lähestymistapa, kun vastaukset näytettiin aina tietyn ajan jälkeen. Mielestäni parempi olisi, että jokaisen olisi annettu tehdä ja ymmärtää itse ja tarvittaessa olisi avustettu. Liian paljon perustui copypasteemiseen, ymmärtämisen sijaan. Mutta toisaalta tositilanteessa jo se, että tietää millä jonkin ongelman voisi ratkaista, on hyvä juttu. Viikkoexamien aukiolot vaihtelivat jatkuvasti, voisi olla jokin selkeä sääntö aukiololle.
