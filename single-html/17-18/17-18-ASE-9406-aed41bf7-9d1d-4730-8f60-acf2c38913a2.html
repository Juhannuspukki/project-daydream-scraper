Nykyinen vastaus
Dear Students, Thank you very much for taking the time of providing us with constructive feedback. Over the years we try to develop the course and have into account your comments and suggestions. At the same, we also need to have into account other wishes by administration, etc. Some times stakeholders are not having a perfect alignment in their wishes :) Many of your critics were regarding the number of credits and the rush of the course, and we needed to do something about it. As first outcome, from next year implementation this corse will be accounting for 5 credit points, that is an increase of a credit point. To change credits in a course is not an easy task and it generates many side effects...basically all the other courses of the FAII needed to change their amount of credits. Regarding the rush time, and the feeling of "many things to do at the same time". We need to recall the course is implemented in one period, meaning you are gathering 5 credit points in half of the time of many of the other courses at TUT...and that is having an effect on the rush and pressure feeling during the course. Next year we will be relax our 3rd assignment working and de ostration because we hope to have another 2 robots working for this assignment, this will increase the options of accessing the cells and work with them. As I explained during the starting of the course I needed to take 4 hours in order to balance previous knowledge for those students who did not take the previous course during last implementation, so I needed to rush with the kinematics modeling, these really took teaching hours previously allocated to the new topics, and probably did unbalance a little bit the programme. In addition, it was the first time we implement the course with these contents, and the main driven force was to also make possible for those students majoring in Robotics to gather certain topics which are no so relevenat for FAII students, so it is clear I need to work better in order to cover the expectations of all students groups. I will try still to keep the majority of the theoretical lectures as they were, so practical knowledge will be moved to assignments, these means at the time of working the assignment we will face also unknown "things" and we will need to be ready to work for those as much as we are ready when they presented during the theoretical lectures. Again, thank you very much for attending the course, and for the feedback
