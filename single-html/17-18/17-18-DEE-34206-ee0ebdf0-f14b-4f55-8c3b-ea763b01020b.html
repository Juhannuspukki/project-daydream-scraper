Nykyinen vastaus
Dear all, Thank you all for the feedback. I saw many of you working quite hard during the course. I was also positively impressed by the exam results. It seemed that most of you could even solve modeling problems that were not presented in the class. This is just what is needed in the industry and what I tried to test in the exam. E.g., inverters with simple L-filters are long-gone history and the real modeling problems are much more complicated, including multiple feedback loops, active damping, adaptive control, communication between converters etc.. However, with solid background you can overcome any obstacle if you just have enough time to dig through. The course was held in one single period to necessitate graduation to MSc within two years, which seems to be the general state guideline. This of course means that there is a lot to do during the seven-week period. Moreover, due to fact that I am not a full-time teacher, I was visiting a conference for one week. Therefore, there was just not enough time for proper recap. I gave some material on the webpage to revise on your own, but of course that is not the same thing. In the future the basic dynamic modeling course will be on the first period and is followed by this course on the second period. This will hopefully solve the need for recap since topics are already in short-term memory. The laboratory work has been improved every year and now it is time again to make the instructions more clear and possibly leave some more time per group to make the measurements. Some of you thought there could be more topics and some had an opposing opinion. I think with the workload we are about to find a balance. The workload is of course a personal experience and depends on the amount of other courses as well. Special emphasis will be made regarding the schedule of the course. Now there might not have been enough time to prepare with questions before every advising hour. This is a challenge since there are only seven weeks to go. I wish you all the best in finalizing your studies and future career as power electronics experts! Best Regards Tuomas Messo
