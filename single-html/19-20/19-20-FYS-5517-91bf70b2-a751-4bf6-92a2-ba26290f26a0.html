Miten kehittäisit opintojaksoa?
Opettajan vastaus :
Thank you for the feedback and I am sorry that the jokes (which I was not aware of making) were sometimes too distracting. This was not intended but might stem from my intent to create a relaxed environment, which I hoped might trigger more discussions and questions. I am very sorry to hear that the exercises were not received that well. I agree that I (we) underestimated some of the exercises before handing them out, however, I tried to be fair by not even increasing the number of achievable points in the rather challenging exercises, which also means that some exercises gave rather easy-to-achieve points. I will try to implement and balance the exercises better in the future. I will also try to formulate the questions and answers in more detail or less ambiguous. That being said, the special circumstances prevented us from having dedicated exercise sessions in which all questions and ambiguities could be clarified. Hence, we learned to implement it in this way also just now and we are sorry if this caused some confusion. Maybe you can give such feedback already during the course next time you encounter these issues, such that we can adjust the exercise session by trying to implement it also as a discussion session and clarify the misunderstandings. Similarly for the systems of pointing in the exercises, please raise these issues during the course and I can react and adapt. Lastly, I am surprised about the feedback that I posed a question in the exam that was apparently too challenging to present during the lecture. This was not the case. There were questions addressing topics for which the exact calculation is very challenging and was not given during the lecture. However, in the exam, I asked for a conceptual understanding of these topics only and no calculation. I also included in the exam some easier problems and some problems that are more challenging on purpose. This was done to give fair chances to all students to show their abilities. Hence, the exam might have been a bit longer than the usual exams (thereby might have occurred more challenging) but this was also caused by the fact that all material had to be allowed. I also adjusted the point scale, i.e. what amount of points are considered to be an excellent exam, to account for that. Julkaistu palaute
Palautteen julkaisi: Fickler, Robert, 09.06.2020
Too much jokes make me hard to focuse on lecture content.
Exercise sessions were awful. It's not only because of online format where students get the exercise sets and solutions after deadline (without online meetings). The teacher answered questions quickly, but he was in doubt if it concerned other ways of approaches to solving a problem. The wording of questions is sometimes very vague. Sometimes too complex problems were proposed, the theory for the solution of which went beyond the framework of the courses. Moreover, the author of the tasks sometimes did not have a ready-made solution in advance, and he himself did not know in advance how difficult the task could be. The teacher should carefully formulate the tasks and solve them in advance in order to assess their complexity. The theory on the exam also went beyond the scope of the course, and some of these tasks were too complex to complete. For example, for one problem, even in the lecture notes, it was noted that the simplified case was difficult to solve and the answer was immediately offered, but in the exam, a more complicated case was proposed for a direct solution. It was proposed for a solution, because the course was not considered, and the answer in the form of the formula was not given. For the next exam, one would not want the questions to go so far beyond the course.
Homework was bit difficult sometimes
The grading system for the exercises seemed arbitrary and not that transparent. Also the given solutions were quite short and sometimes incomplete. Due to the lack of exercise sessions for discussion, I would have expected a bit more detail in the solutions and the grading criteria on Moodle. There was also an unusual amount of errors in the given exercise solutions, that were quietly fixed throughout the course. A bit more transparency in pointing out these errors and their effect on the exercise scores would have been good. Finally, I would have personally restricted the exam to only a cheat sheet to even out the playing field with previous implementations. But there was no good solution to this problem.
The grading of exercises could improve. Now, it does not really correspond to the amount of work needed to solve the problem. The worst contrast was of course at exercise set 5 where students get 10 points from two easy calculations and 3 points from a difficult (Robert Boyd's words) derivation. In the future, the amount of points awarded from each problem should reflect more on the amount of work required.
Exercise sessions need to be improved. The tasks were not corresponding to the materials from lectures at times; the problems might sound ambiguous and unclear (or missing some data making the task confusing).
